10.10 Exercises — Absolute and Conditional Convergence

For problems 1–7, determine whether the following series converge absolutely, converge conditionally, or diverge.

(1) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{1 + n} \)

(2) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n + 1}}{\sqrt{n + 1}} \)

(3) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n - 1} \frac{n}{n^2 + 1} \)

(4) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 2}^{\infty} (-3)^{n} \frac{n}{3^{n} (n^3 - 2n)} \)

(5) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\csc(n)}{n^2} \)

(6) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 2}^{\infty} \frac{2}{\tan(n)} \)

(7) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 3}^{\infty} \frac{3}{\ln(n)} \)

For problems 8–12, find the values of \(x\) for which the following series converge conditionally.

(8) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{n^x}{n^2 - 4} \)

(9) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{(2x - 3)n}{8n^2 - 2n + 5}\)

(10) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 4}^{\infty} \frac{(5x -3)^n}{(-2)^n (n+2)} \)

(11) \( \displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\left( \frac{x}{4} \right)^n}{n \cos(\pi n)} \)

Problems 12–17 are multiple-choice questions. Select the best answer from the choices provided.

(12) Consider the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\cos(\pi n)}{n^p} \). What are the values of \(p\) for which the series converges conditionally?

(A) \(1 \leq p \lt \infty \)

(B) \(1 \lt p \lt \infty \)

(C) \( -\infty \lt p \lt 1 \)

(D) \(0 \le p \lt 1 \)

(E) \(0 \lt p \leq 1 \)

(13) Which of the following statements is true?

(A) If \(\displaystyle \sum |a_n|\) diverges, then so does \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\).

(B) If \(\displaystyle \sum (-1)^n a_n\) converges, then so does \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\).

(C) If \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) converges, then so does \(\displaystyle \sum (-1)^n a_n\).

(D) If \(\displaystyle \sum |a_n|\) converges, then so does \(\displaystyle \sum (-1)^n a_n \).

(E) Answer choices (C) and (D) are correct.

(14) Consider the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \cos(\pi n) \frac{n^9}{1 + n^{11}} \). Which of the following is true?

(A) The series converges absolutely and conditionally.

(B) The series converges conditionally.

(C) The series converges absolutely.

(D) The series diverges.

(E) The series diverges conditionally.

(15) Which of the following series converge(s) conditionally?

I. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{3n^2 - 8}\)

II. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\sin(n-4)}{7n - 3} \)

III. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{2n}{e^n} \)

(A) I only

(B) II only

(C) III only

(D) I and II only

(E) II and III only

(16) Which of the following series converge(s) absolutely?

I. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\cos(\pi n)}{3n + 2}\)

II. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{n + 6}{n^3 (-1)^n} \)

III. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} e^{-\sqrt{n}} \)

(A) I only

(B) II only

(C) III only

(D) II and III only

(E) I, II, and III

(17) It is known that \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^{\infty} a_n \) converges. It is also known that \(a_n > 0\) for all \(n \geq 0\). Consider the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^{\infty} f(n) a_n\), and select the FALSE statement below.

(A) For \(\displaystyle f(n) = \sin{\left( \frac{\pi}{2} + kn \right)}\), where \(k \in \mathbb{Z} \), the series converges absolutely.

(B) For \(\displaystyle f(n) = \frac{1}{q(n) + 2}\), where \(\displaystyle q(n)\) is a polynomial of degree \(\gt 0\) such that \(q(n) + 2 \ne 0\), the series converges.

(C) For \(\displaystyle f(n) = \cos( \pi n )\), the series converges conditionally.

(D) For \(\displaystyle f(n) = n,\) the convergence of the series cannot be determined.

(E) None of the above.

Problems 18–22 are conceptual questions that require you to explain your reasoning.

(18) Explain why a series cannot be both conditionally convergent and absolutely convergent.

(19) Try to think of a series such that \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) converges but that \(\displaystyle \sum |a_n|\) diverges. (Note—if you cannot think of any, then think about why.)

(20) Explain why the ratio test is inconclusive when \(\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} \left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right| = 1\).
