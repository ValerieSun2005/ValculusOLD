5.5 Exercises — Problem 10

\((10)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_4^\infty \frac{3}{x \ln(x)} \, \textrm{d} x \) or show that it diverges.

We will rewrite the integral as $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_4^k \frac{3}{x \ln(x)} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Notice that we can perform the substitution \(u = \ln(x)\) to find an antiderivative. Doing so, \(\displaystyle \textrm{d} u = \frac{1}{x} \, \textrm{d} x\). $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_4^k \frac{3}{x \ln(x)} \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_{x = 4}^{x = k} \frac{3}{u} \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = 3 \lim_{k \to \infty} \ln|u| \Big]_{x = 4}^{x = k} $$ $$ = 3 \lim_{k \to \infty} \ln\left|\ln(x)\right| \Big]_4^k $$ $$ = 3 \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \ln\left|\ln(k)\right|}_{\infty} - 3 \ln(\ln(4)) $$ Because \(\displaystyle \lim_{k \to \infty} \ln\left|\ln(k)\right|\) does not exist (it approaches infinity, although very slowly), $$ \int_4^\infty \frac{3}{x \ln(x)} \, \textrm{d} x \, \, \, \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\textrm{diverges}.} $$
