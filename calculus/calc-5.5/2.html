5.5 Exercises — Problem 2

\((2)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{3}{2\sqrt{x}} \, \textrm{d} x \) or show that it diverges.

This integral is a Type I improper integral. We will insert a variable \(k\) for the upper bound and evaluate the limit as it approaches infinity. We will then obtain $$ \int_1^\infty \frac{3}{2\sqrt{x}} \, \textrm{d} x = \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_1^k\frac{3}{2\sqrt{x}} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Antidifferentiating, we obtain $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{3}{2} \int_1^k x^{-1/2} \, \textrm{d} x$$ $$ = \frac{3}{2} \lim_{k \to \infty} \left(2 \sqrt{x} \right) \Big]_1^k $$ $$ = 3 \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \sqrt{k}}_{\infty} - 3(1). $$ Because \(\displaystyle \lim_{k \to \infty} \sqrt{k} \) does not exist, $$ \int_2^\infty \frac{3}{2 \sqrt{x}} \, \textrm{d} x \, \, \, \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\textrm{diverges}.} $$
