5.5 Exercises — Problem 20

\((20)\) Determine whether \(\displaystyle \int_{-1}^3 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), \( \displaystyle f(x)= \begin{cases} 2x & \text{for $ x \leq 0$} \\[2ex] x^2 & \text{for $ x \gt 0, $} \end{cases} \) is a proper or improper integral. If it is improper, classify the type (i.e, Type I or Type II).

We notice that $$ \lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x) = 0; $$ $$ \lim_{x \to 0^+} f(x) = 0, $$ which are equivalent. Therefore, \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 0\). We integrate from \(x = -1\) to \(x = 3\). \(f\) is continuous for all other \(x\), so this integral is proper.
