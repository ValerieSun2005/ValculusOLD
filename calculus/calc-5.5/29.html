5.5 Exercises — Problem 29

\((29)\) Indicate whether the following statement is true: If \(f(x)\) is continuous, positive, and decreasing for all \(x \geq 1\), and \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 0\), then \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty f(x)\) converges.

Consider \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\). It is continuous, positive, and decreasing for all \(x \geq 1\), and \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0 \). However, we understand that \(\displaystyle \int_a^\infty \frac{1}{x} \, \textrm{d} x\), where \(a \gt 0\), diverges. Therefore, the statement is false.
