5.5 Exercises — Problem 6

\((6)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(x + 3)^2} \, \textrm{d} x \) or show that it diverges.

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_0^k \frac{1}{(x + 3)^2} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Antidifferentiating and plugging in the bounds give $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{-1}{x + 3} \Big]_0^{\infty} $$ $$ = \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{-1}{k + 3}}_{0} + \frac{1}{3} $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{3}.}$$
