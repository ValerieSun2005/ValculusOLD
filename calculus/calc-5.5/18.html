5.5 Exercises — Problem 18

\((18)\) Determine whether \(\displaystyle \int_0^{1/2} \frac{-2}{x^9 - 1} \, \textrm{d} x \) is a proper or improper integral. If it is improper, classify the type (i.e, Type I or Type II).

Notice that \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{x^9 - 1}\) is continuous for all \(x \ne 1\). The bounds of the integral are from \(x = 0\) to \(x = 1/2\), which does not include this discontinuity. Therefore, the integral is proper.
