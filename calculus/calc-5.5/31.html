5.5 Exercises — Problem 31

\((31)\) The density of trees in a forest, \(T\), in terms of the distance, \(r\), surrounding a campsite is given by \(\displaystyle T(r) = \frac{290}{r^3 + 8r^2 + 16r}\), where \(r\) is measured in meters and \(T(r)\) is measured in trees per square meter. The number of trees within \(r\) meters of the campsite is given by \(\displaystyle 2 \pi \int_0^r u T(u) \, \textrm{d} u\). Explain why the total number of trees in the forest is less than \(500\).

We will consider \(\displaystyle 2 \pi \int_0^\infty r T(r) \, \textrm{d} r\) (note that \(u\) was simply a dummy variable.) In practicality, however, the forest is likely to end—it would be impossible for it to cover an infinite distance. Therefore, the value given by the improper integral serves as an upper bound to the total number of trees in the forest, which is assumed to span a finite distance.

Nevertheless, we consider $$ 2 \pi \int_0^\infty rT(r) \, \textrm{d} r $$ $$ = 2 \pi \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_0^k \frac{290}{r^2 + 8r + 16} \, \textrm{d} r $$ $$ = 580 \pi \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_0^k \frac{1}{(r + 4)^2} \, \textrm{d} r $$ $$ = 580 \pi \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{-1}{r + 4} \Big]_0^k $$ $$ = 580 \pi \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{-1}{k + 4}}_{0} - 580 \pi \cdot \frac{-1}{0 + 4} $$ $$ = \frac{580 \pi}{4} \lt 500. $$
