5.5 Exercises — Problem 11

\((11)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_{-\pi/2}^2 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), \( \displaystyle f(x)= \begin{cases} \sin(x) & \text{for $x \leq 0 $} \\[2ex] x & \text{for $ x \gt 0 $}, \end{cases} \) or show that it diverges.

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int_{-\pi/2}^0 f(x) \textrm{d} x + \int_0^2 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x; $$ you may verify that these integrals are proper and convergent (\(f\) is continuous at \(x = 0\).) Evaluating each integral, $$ \int_{-\pi/2}^0 f(x) \textrm{d} x = \int_{-\pi/2}^0 \sin(x) \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = - \cos(x) \Big]_{- \pi/2}^0 $$ $$ = - \cos(0) + \cos{\left(-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)} $$ $$ = -1; $$ $$ \int_0^2 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x = \int_0^2 x \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \frac{x^2}{2} \Big]_0^2 $$ $$ = \frac{2^2}{2} - \frac{0^2}{2} $$ $$ = 2. $$ Therefore, $$ \int_{-\pi/2}^2 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x = -1 + 2 = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{1.} $$
