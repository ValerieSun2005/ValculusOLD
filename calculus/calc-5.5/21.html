5.5 Exercises — Problem 21

\((21)\) Determine whether \(\displaystyle \int_{-\pi}^{\infty} f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), \( \displaystyle f(x)= \begin{cases} \cos(x) + 1 & \text{for $ x \leq 0$} \\[2ex] x^2 + 1 & \text{for $ x \gt 0, $} \end{cases} \) is a proper or improper integral. If it is improper, classify the type (i.e, Type I or Type II).

We notice that $$ \lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x) = 0; $$ $$ \lim_{x \to 0^+} f(x) = 0, $$ which are equivalent. Therefore, \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 0\). However, the limits of integration are from \(-\pi\) to \(\infty\). Since \(f\) is continuous for \(-\pi \leq x \lt \infty\), this integral is a Type I improper integral.
