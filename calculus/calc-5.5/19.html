5.5 Exercises — Problem 19

\((19)\) Determine whether \(\displaystyle \int_0^8 f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), \( \displaystyle f(x)= \begin{cases} 3 & \text{for $0 \leq x \leq 2$} \\[2ex] -7 & \text{for $ x \gt 2, $} \end{cases} \) is a proper or improper integral. If it is improper, classify the type (i.e, Type I or Type II).

We notice that \(f\) has a discontinuity at \(x = 2\), as $$ \lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = 3 $$ $$ \lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = -7, $$ which are not equivalent. Therefore, because the integral bounds are from \(x = 0\) to \(x = 8\), the integral is improper (Type II) but convergent, as you may verify.
