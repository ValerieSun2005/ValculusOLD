5.5 Exercises — Problem 3

\((3)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_3^\infty \frac{1}{x\sqrt[4]{x}} \, \textrm{d} x \) or show that it diverges.

First, we notice that \(\displaystyle x \sqrt[4]{x} = x \cdot x^{1/4} = x^{5/4} \). Thus, the integral becomes $$ \int_3^\infty \frac{1}{x^{5/4}} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ This integral is a Type I improper integral. We will insert a variable \(k\) for the upper bound and evaluate the limit as it approaches infinity. We will then obtain $$ \int_3^\infty \frac{1}{x^{5/4}} \, \textrm{d} x = \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_3^k x^{-5/4} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Antidifferentiating gives $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \left(-4 x^{-1/4} \right) \Big]_3^\infty $$ $$ = -4 \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt[4]{k}}}_{0} - \left( \frac{-4}{\sqrt[4]{3}} \right) $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{4}{\sqrt[4]{3}}.} $$
