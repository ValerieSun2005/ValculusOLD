5.5 Exercises — Problem 25

\((25)\) It is known that \(\displaystyle 0 \lt f(x) \leq \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \), where \(f\) is continuous for all \(x\). Show that \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x \) converges and is no greater than \(3\).

Since \(\displaystyle 0 \lt f(x) \leq \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \) and \(f\) is continuous for all \(x \geq 1\), it follows that $$ 0 \lt \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x \leq \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ We notice that \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, \textrm{d} x\) is a convergent \(p\)-integral (\(p = \frac{4}{3}\)). Therefore, using the comparison test, \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\) converges too. Furthermore, the value of \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, \textrm{d} x\) can be used to bound \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x\).

To determine the value of \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, \textrm{d} x\), we will first insert a variable, \(k\), for the upper bound and take the limit as it approaches infinity. We get $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} \int_1^k x^{-4/3} \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to \infty} -3 x^{-1/3} \Big]_1^k $$ $$ = -3 \underbrace{\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{k}}}_{0} + 3 \frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{1}} $$ $$ = 3. $$ Therefore, $$ 0 \lt \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x \leq \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, \textrm{d} x. $$ $$ 0 \lt \int_1^\infty f(x) \, \textrm{d} x \leq 3. $$
