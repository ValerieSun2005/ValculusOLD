5.5 Exercises — Problem 12

\((12)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_{-2}^3 g(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), \( \displaystyle g(x)= \begin{cases} x + 5 & \text{for $-2 \leq x \leq 0$} \\[2ex] \ln(x) & \text{for $0 \lt x \leq 4$}, \end{cases} \) or show that it diverges.

Notice that \(g\) contains a discontinuity at \(x = 0\). However, if we rewrite the integral as $$ \int_{-2}^0 g(x) \, \textrm{d} x + \int_0^3 g(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int_{-2}^0 \left(x + 5\right) \, \textrm{d} x + \int_0^3 \ln(x) \, \textrm{d} x, $$ we immediately notice that \(\displaystyle \int_{-2}^0 \left(x + 5\right) \, \textrm{d} x\) is convergent and equal to \(8\). If \(\displaystyle \int_0^3 \ln(x) \, \textrm{d} x\) is convergent too, then \(\displaystyle \int_{-2}^3 g(x) \, \textrm{d} x\) will be convergent.

To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^3 \ln(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), recall that $$ \int \ln(x) = x \ln(x) - x + C. $$ Furthermore, notice that \(\ln(x)\) is undefined for \(x \leq 0\); therefore, we are working with a Type II integral. Thus, we obtain $$ \lim_{k \to 0^+} \int_k^3 \ln(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to 0^+} \left(x \ln(x) - x\right)\Big]_k^3 $$ $$ = \left( 3 \ln(3) - 3 \right) - \left(\lim_{k \to 0^+} \left[k \ln(k) - k\right] \right) $$ $$ = \left( 3 \ln(3) - 3 \right) - \lim_{k \to 0^+} k \ln(k). $$ Notice that, for \(\displaystyle \lim_{k \to 0^+} k \ln(k)\), \(\displaystyle \lim_{k \to 0^+} k = 0\) and \(\displaystyle \lim_{k \to 0^+} k \ln(k) = -\infty\). We are therefore in a \(0 \cdot \infty\) indeterminate form, for which we may resort to L'Hôpital's Rule: $$ \lim_{k \to 0^+} k \ln(k) $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{\ln(k)}{1/k}, $$ which is in the indeterminate form \(\displaystyle \frac{\infty}{\infty}\). We can then differentiate the numerator and denominator to get $$ \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{\textrm{d}}{\textrm{d} k} \ln(k)}{ \frac{\textrm{d}}{\textrm{d} k} 1/k} $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{1/k}{-1/k^2} $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to 0^+} -k $$ $$ = 0. $$ Therefore, $$ \int_0^3 \ln(x) \, \textrm{d} x = 3\ln(3) - 3. $$ We conclude that $$ \int_{-2}^3 g(x) \, \textrm{d} x = 8 + \left(3\ln(3) - 3\right)$$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{5 + 3 \ln(3).} $$
