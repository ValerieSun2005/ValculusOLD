Problem 8 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/4} \theta \cos(4 \theta) \, \dd \theta. \)

SOLUTION Using the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = \theta\) and \(\dd v = \cos(4 \theta) \, \dd \theta.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd \theta\) and \(v = \frac{1}{4} \sin(4 \theta).\) After plugging in these expressions, we obtain \[ \begin{aligned} \int_0^{\pi/4} \theta \cos(4 \theta) \, \dd \theta &= \frac{1}{4} \theta \sin(4 \theta) \Big|_0^{\pi/4} - \frac{1}{4} \int_0^{\pi/4} \sin(4 \theta) \, \dd \theta \\ &= \frac{1}{4} \theta \sin(4 \theta) \Big|_0^{\pi/4} + \frac{1}{16} \cos(4 \theta) \Big|_0^{\pi/4} \\ &= \left(\frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \sin(\pi) - \frac{1}{4}(0) \sin(0) \right) + \left( \frac{1}{16} \cos(\pi) - \frac{1}{16} \cos(0) \right) \\ &= \Big(0\Big) + \left(-\frac{1}{8}\right) \\ &= \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{- \frac{1}{8}.} \end{aligned} \]
