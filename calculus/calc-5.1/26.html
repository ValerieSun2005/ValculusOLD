Problem 26 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int Q^2 e^Q \left(\sin Q - \cos Q \right) \, \dd Q. \)

SOLUTION Using the formula for integration by parts, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = Q^2\) and \(\dd v = e^Q(\sin Q - \cos Q) \, \dd Q.\) Notice that, from the power rule, \[\frac{\dd}{\dd Q} \left(-e^Q \cos(Q)\right) = e^Q(\sin Q - \cos Q).\] Therefore, integrating \(\dd v\) will reverse the power rule. Consequently, \(\dd u = 2 Q \, \dd Q\) and \(v = -e^Q \cos(Q).\) Plugging these expressions into the formula yield \[ \int Q^2 e^Q \left(\sin Q - \cos Q \right) \, \dd Q = -Q^2 e^Q \cos(Q) + 2 \int Qe^Q \cos(Q) \, \dd Q. \] At this point, in order to evaluate \(\displaystyle \int Q e^Q \cos(Q) \, \dd Q,\) we must apply integration by parts again. From the formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we will select \(u = Q\) and \(\dd v = e^Q \cos(Q) \, \dd Q.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd Q\) and \(\displaystyle v = \frac{1}{2} e^Q (\sin Q + \cos Q). \) (Note that these choices of \(u\) and \(\dd v\) are new and not to be confused with the previous iteration.) The integral is therefore re-expressed as \[\frac{1}{2} Q e^Q(\sin Q + \cos Q) - \frac{1}{2} \int e^Q (\sin Q + \cos Q) \, \dd Q. \] Note that \[\frac{\dd}{\dd Q} (e^Q \sin Q + C) = e^Q (\sin Q + \cos Q) \implies \int e^Q (\sin Q + \cos Q) \, \dd Q = e^Q \sin(Q) + C. \] Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} \int Q e^Q \cos(Q) \, \dd Q &= \frac{1}{2} Q e^Q(\sin Q + \cos Q) - \frac{1}{2} \int e^Q (\sin Q + \cos Q) \, \dd Q \\ &= \frac{1}{2} Q e^Q(\sin Q + \cos Q) - \frac{1}{2} e^Q \sin(Q) + C. \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \int Q^2 e^Q \left(\sin Q - \cos Q \right) \, \dd Q &= -Q^2 e^Q \cos(Q) + 2 \int Qe^Q \cos(Q) \, \dd Q. \\ &= \box{-Q^2 e^Q \cos(Q) + Q e^Q(\sin Q + \cos Q) - e^Q \sin(Q) + C.} \end{aligned} \]
