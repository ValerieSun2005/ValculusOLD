Problem 18 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \cos^3(\theta) 4^{\sin \theta } \, \dd \theta.\)

SOLUTION For this problem, we will perform the substitution \(k = \sin(\theta),\) as we will then get \(\dd k = \cos(\theta) \, \dd \theta.\) The integral then becomes \[ \begin{aligned} &\int \cos^2(\theta) 4^k \, \dd k \\ = &\int \left(1 - \sin^2 \theta \right) e^{k \ln 4 } \, \dd k \\ = &\int \left(1 - k^2\right) e^{k \ln 4} \, \dd k. \end{aligned} \]

At this point we will perform tabular integration, electing to differentiate \(1 - k^2\) and integrate \(e^{k \ln 4}.\)



Pairing the expressions on either side of each arrow—noting the signs—gives \[ \frac{1}{\ln(4)} \left(1 - k^2\right) \cdot 4^k + \frac{2}{\ln^2(4)} k \cdot 4^k - \frac{2}{\ln^3(4)} \cdot 4^k + C. \\ \] Substituting back yields \[ \box {\frac{1}{\ln(4)} \left(1 - \sin^2 \theta \right) \cdot 4^{\sin \theta} + \frac{2}{\ln^2(4)} \sin(\theta) \cdot 4^{\sin \theta} - \frac{2}{\ln^3(4)} \cdot 4^{\sin \theta} + C.} \]
