Problem 19 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \frac{x \sin^{-1}(x)}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, \dd x. \)

SOLUTION For this problem, we first perform the substitution \(k = \sin^{-1}(x)\) because then \(\displaystyle \dd k = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, \dd x \) and \(x = \sin(k).\) The integral then becomes \[\int k \sin(k) \, \dd k.\] Using the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = k\) and \(\dd v = \sin(k) \, \dd k. \) Then, \(\dd u = \dd k\) and \(v = -cos(k).\) Substituting these expressions in yields \[ \begin{aligned} \int k \sin(k) \, \dd k &= -k \cos(k) + \int \cos(k) \, \dd k \\ &= -k \cos(k) + \sin(k) + C. \end{aligned} \] Substituting back gives \[-\sin^{-1}(x) \cos \left(\sin^{-1} x \right) + \sin \left(\sin^{-1} x \right) + C. \] We understand that, for \(-1 \leq x \leq 1,\) \(\sin \left(\sin^{-1} x \right) = x.\) Additionally, to simplify \(\cos \left(\sin^{-1} x \right),\) we draw a triangle. \(\sin^{-1} x, \) which we will denote \(\theta,\) represents the angle of a right triangle with opposite length \(x\) and hypotenuse \(1\).



Therefore, \[\cos \left(\sin^{-1} x \right) = \cos(\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}{1} = \sqrt{1 - x^2}. \] We then have—for our final answer— \[\box{-\sin^{-1}(x) \sqrt{1 - x^2} + x + C.}\]
