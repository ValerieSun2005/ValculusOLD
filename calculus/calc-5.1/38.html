Problem 38 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Using the substitution \(k = \ln(x),\) the integral \(\displaystyle \int \ln(x) \, \dd x\) can be re-expressed as \(\displaystyle \int k e^k \, \dd k.\) Show this process and, using integration by parts, verify that the result is the expected expression \(x \ln(x) - x + C.\)

SOLUTION The integral can be rewritten as \[\int \frac{x}{x} \ln(x) \, \dd x.\] At this point, we can perform the substitution \(k = \ln(x).\) Then, \(\displaystyle \dd k = \frac{1}{x} \, \dd x\) and \(x = e^k.\) The integral then becomes \[\int k e^k \, \dd k,\] for which we can perform integration by parts. Referencing the formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we choose \(u = k\) and \(\dd v = e^k \, \dd k.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd k\) and \(v = e^k.\) Therefore, plugging in these expressions gives \[ke^k - \int e^k \, \dd k = k e^k - e^k + C.\] Substituting back gives \[x\ln(x) - x + C,\] as expected.
