Problem 24 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \ln \left(\ln \sin \theta \right) \ln^3(\sin \theta) \cot \theta \, \dd \theta. \)

SOLUTION We can rewrite the integral, after noticing that \(\displaystyle \cot \theta = \cos \theta/ \sin \theta,\) as \[\int \frac{\ln \left(\ln \sin \theta \right) \ln(\sin \theta)^3 \cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \, \dd \theta,\] for which we can perform the substitution \(u = \sin \theta.\) Then, \(\dd u = \cos \theta \, \dd \theta\) and the integral becomes \[\int \frac{\ln \left(\ln u \right) \ln(u)^3}{u} \, \dd u. \] At this point, we can further rewrite the integral as \[ \begin{aligned} &\int \frac{\ln \left(\ln u \right) \ln(u)^4}{u \ln(u)} \, \dd u \\ =&\int \frac{\ln \left(\ln u \right) e^{4 \ln(\ln u)}}{u \ln(u)} \, \dd u. \end{aligned} \] We now perform the substitution \(v = \ln(\ln u ).\) Then, \(\displaystyle \dd v = 1/(u \ln u) \, \dd u\) and the integral becomes \[\int ve^{4v} \, \dd v,\] for which we can perform integration by parts. Using the (alternatively-notated) formula for integration by parts, \(\int fg' \, \dd x = fg - \int f' g \, \dd x,\) we let \(f = v\) and \(g' = e^{4v}.\) Then, \(f' = 1\) and \(g = \frac{1}{4} e^{4v}.\) Plugging in these expressions yields \[ \begin{aligned} &\frac{1}{4} ve^{4v} - \int \frac{1}{4} e^{4v} \, \dd x \\ = &\frac{1}{4} ve^{4v} - \frac{1}{16} e^{4v} + C. \\ = &\frac{1}{4} \ln(\ln u) \ln(u)^4 - \frac{1}{16} \ln(u)^4 + C. \\ = &\frac{1}{4} \ln(\ln \sin \theta) \ln(\sin \theta)^4 - \frac{1}{16} \ln(\sin \theta)^4 + C. \end{aligned} \]
