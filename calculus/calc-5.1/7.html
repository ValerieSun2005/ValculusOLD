Problem 7 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \frac{x - 1}{e^{x}} \, \dd x. \)

SOLUTION We can rewrite the integral as \[ \begin{aligned} \int \left( \frac{x}{e^x} - \frac{1}{e^x} \right) \, \dd x &= \int xe^{-x} \, \dd x - \int e^{-x} \, \dd x \\ &= e^{-x} + \int xe^{-x} \, \dd x. \end{aligned} \] To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int xe^{-x} \, \dd x,\) we will choose to differentiate \(x\) and integrate \(e^{-x}.\) Using the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = x\) and \(\dd v = e^{-x} \, \dd x.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd x\) and \(v = -e^{-x}.\) We then have—after plugging in these expressions— \[ \begin{aligned} \int xe^{-x} \, \dd x &= -xe^{-x} + \int e^{-x} \, \dd x \\ &= -xe^{-x} - e^{-x} + C. \end{aligned} \]

We now have \[ \begin{aligned} e^{-x} - \int xe^{-x} \, \dd x &= e^{-x} + \left(-xe^{-x} - e^{-x}\right) + C \\ &=\bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{xe^{-x} + C.} \end{aligned} \]
