Problem 32 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Let \(k\) be a twice-differentiable function whose second derivative is positive for all \(x.\) It is known that \(k(0) = 0, k(2) = 3,\) and \(k'(2) = 4.\) Show that \(\displaystyle 0 \lt \int_0^2 x \sin(x) k''(x) \, \dd x \leq 5.\)

SOLUTION For \(0 \lt x \lt 2,\) \(x\) and \(\sin(x)\) are both positive. We are also given that \(k''(x) \gt 0\) for all \(x.\) Since we are dealing with a positive integrand and \(|\sin(x)| \leq 1\) for all \(x,\) \[0 \lt \int_0^2 x \sin(x) k''(x) \, \dd x \leq \int_0^2 x k''(x) \, \dd x.\] To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^2 x k''(x) \, \dd x,\) we will perform integration by parts. Referencing the formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we choose \(u = x\) and \(\dd v = k''(x) \, \dd x.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd k\) and \(v = k'(x).\) We then have \[ \begin{aligned} \int_0^2 x k'(x) \, \dd x &= x k'(x) \Big|_0^2 - \int_0^2 k'(x) \, \dd x \\ &= \Big( 2k'(2) - 0 \Big) - \Big(k(2) - k(0)\Big) \\ &= 2(4) - \left(3 - 0\right) \\ &= 5. \end{aligned} \]

Therefore, the inequality becomes \[0 \lt \int_0^2 x \sin(x) k''(x) \, \dd x \leq 5.\]
