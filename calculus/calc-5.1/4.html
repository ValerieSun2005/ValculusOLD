Problem 4 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int x e^{7x} \, \dd x. \)

SOLUTION We can utilize the formula for integration by parts, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u, \] to re-express the integral. We will choose \(u = x\) and \(\dd v = e^{7x} \, \dd x.\) Then, \(\displaystyle \dd u = \, \dd x\) and \(\displaystyle v = \frac{1}{7} e^{7x}. \) We then have \[ \begin{aligned} \int x e^{7x} \, \dd x &= \frac{1}{7} xe^{7x} - \frac{1}{7} \int e^{7x} \, \dd x \\ &= \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{7} xe^{7x} - \frac{1}{49} e^{7x} + C.} \end{aligned} \]
