Problem 10 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \frac{\ln(\ln x)}{x} \, \dd x. \)

SOLUTION We will first perform the substitution \(k = \ln(x)\) because then \(\displaystyle \dd k = \frac{1}{x} \, \dd x\) and the integral becomes \[\int \ln(k) \, \dd k.\] Using the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = \ln(k) \) and \(\dd v = \, \dd k.\) Then, \(\displaystyle \dd u = \frac{1}{k} \, \dd k\) and \(v = k.\) Plugging in these expressions yields \[ \begin{aligned} \ln(k) \, \dd k &= k \ln(k) - \int (k) \left(\frac{1}{k} \, \dd k \right) \\ &= k \ln(k) - \int \, \dd k \\ &= k \ln(k) - k + C. \end{aligned} \] We must now substitute back, recalling that \(k = \ln(x).\) We then get \[\bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\ln(x) \ln(\ln x) - \ln(x) + C.}\]
