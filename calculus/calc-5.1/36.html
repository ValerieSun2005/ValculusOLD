Problem 36 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

The rate at which the number of followers of a Twitter user changes as a function of time, \(t,\) is given by \(\ell(t) = 10 - te^{-t/40},\) where \(t\) is measured in hours and \(\ell(t)\) is measured in followers per hour. The user initially has \(30\) followers. Find how many followers the user will have after \(6\) hours.

SOLUTION The number of followers the user will have after \(6\) hours is given by \[30 + \int_0^6 \ell(t) \, \dd t.\] To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^6 (10 - te^{-t/40}) \, \dd t,\) we apply linearity by splitting it up as \(\displaystyle \int_0^6 10 \, \dd t - \int_0^6 te^{-t/40} \, \dd t.\) At this point, to evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^6 te^{-t/40} \, \dd t,\) we will perform integration by parts. Referencing the formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we choose \(u = t\) and \(\dd v = e^{-t/40} \, \dd t.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd t\) and \(v = -40 e^{-t/40},\) and we obtain \[\int_0^6 te^{-t/40} \, \dd t = -40t e^{-t/40} \Big|_0^6 + 40 \int_0^6 e^{-t/40} \, \dd t. \] Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} 30 + \int_0^6 \ell(t) \, \dd t &= 30 + \int_0^6 (10 - te^{-t/40}) \, \dd t \\ &= 30 + \int_0^6 10 \, \dd t - \left(-40t e^{-t/40} \Big|_0^6 + 40 \int_0^6 e^{-t/40} \, \dd t \right)\\ &= 30 + 10(6 - 0) - \Big(-40\left[6e^{-6/40} - 0\right] - 1600 \left[e^{-6/40} - e^0\right] \Big) \\ &= 1840e^{-6/40} -1540 \\ &\approx \box{73.7 \rightarrow 74.} \end{aligned} \]

The user will have approximately \(74\) followers after \(6\) hours.
