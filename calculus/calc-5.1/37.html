Problem 37 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

The velocity of a car traveling on a straight road is given by \(\displaystyle v(t) = 30 - t^2 e^{-t/6},\) where \(t\) is measured in seconds and \(v(t)\) is measured in meters per second. The car is originally located \(400\) meters from Town \(A.\) Derive an expression for the distance of the car from Town \(A\) as a function of \(t.\)

SOLUTION The distance, \(d(t),\) of the car from Town \(A\) as a function of time is given by \[d(t) = 400 + \int_0^t v(x) \, \dd x.\] (Note that \(x\) is simply a dummy variable.) To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^t (30 - x^2 e^{-x/6}) \, \dd x,\) we can apply linearity and apply integration by parts to get \[\int_0^t 30 \, \dd x - \int_0^t x^2 e^{-x/6} \, \dd x. \] To evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_0^t x^2 e^{-x/6} \, \dd x,\) we will utilize tabular integration.



Pairing the expressions on either side of each arrow (and being mindful of the signs, as always) yields \[\int_0^t x^2 e^{-x/6} \, \dd x = -6x^2 e^{-x/6} - 72xe^{-x/6} - 432 e^{-x/6}. \] Therefore, we have \[ \begin{aligned} d(t) &= 400 + \int_0^t 30 \, \dd x - \int_0^x x^2 e^{-x/6} \, \dd x \\ &= 400 + 30x \Big|_0^t - \left(-6x^2 e^{-x/6} - 72xe^{-x/6} - 432 e^{-x/6}\right)\Big|_0^t \\ &= \box{400 + 30t + 6t^2 e^{-t/6} + 72te^{-t/6} + 432 e^{-t/6}.} \\ \end{aligned} \]
