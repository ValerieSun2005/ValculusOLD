Problem 39 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Show that \(\displaystyle \int \cos^n(\theta) \, \dd \theta = \frac{1}{n} \sin(\theta) \cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + \frac{n - 1}{n} \int \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta. \)

SOLUTION Referencing the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we choose \(u = \cos^{n - 1}(\theta)\) and \(\dd v = \cos(\theta) \, \dd \theta.\) Then, \(\dd u = -(n - 1) \cos^{n - 2} \sin(\theta) \, \dd \theta\) and \(v = \sin(\theta).\) We then get \[ \begin{aligned} \int \cos^n(\theta) \, \dd \theta &= \sin(\theta)\cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + (n - 1) \int \sin^2(\theta) \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta \\ &= \sin(\theta)\cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + (n - 1) \int (1 - \cos^2(\theta)) \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta \\ &= \sin(\theta)\cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + (n - 1) \int \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta - (n - 1)\int \cos^n(\theta) \, \dd \theta \\ n \int \cos^n(\theta) \, \dd \theta &= \sin(\theta)\cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + (n - 1) \int \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta \\ \int \cos^n(\theta) \, \dd \theta &= \frac{1}{n} \sin(\theta)\cos^{n - 1}(\theta) + \frac{n - 1}{n} \int \cos^{n - 2}(\theta) \, \dd \theta. \end{aligned} \]
