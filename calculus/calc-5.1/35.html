Problem 35 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

The density of sprinkles on a circular cake as a function of the distance, \(r,\) from the center of the cake is given by \(\displaystyle s(r) = \frac{1}{10}e^{r/3},\) where \(r\) is measured in inches and \(s(r)\) is measured in sprinkles per square inch. The total number of sprinkles on the cake is given by the expression \(\displaystyle \int_0^{10} 2 \pi r s(r) \, \dd r.\) Find this value.

SOLUTION We want to evaluate \[\int_0^{10} 2 \pi r s(r) \, \dd r = \frac{\pi}{5} \int_0^{10} r e^{r/3} \, \dd r. \] Referencing the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u, \] we pick \(u = r\) and \(\dd v = e^{r/3} \, \dd r.\) Then, \(\dd u = \dd r\) and \(v = 3 e^{r/3}.\) We now have \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\pi}{5} \int_0^{10} r e^{r/3} \, \dd r &= \frac{\pi}{5} \left(3re^{r/3} \Big|_0^{10} - 3 \int_0^{10} e^{r/3} \, \dd r \right) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{5} \left(3(10)e^{10/3} - 0 \right) - \frac{9 \pi}{5} \left(e^{10/3} - e^{0}\right) \\ &= 6 \pi e^{10/3} - \frac{9 \pi}{5} e^{10/3} + \frac{9 \pi}{5} \\ &= \frac{21 \pi}{5} e^{10/3} + \frac{9 \pi}{5} \\ &\approx \box{375.52 \rightarrow 376.} \end{aligned} \] There are approximately \(376\) sprinkles on the cake.
