Problem 9 — 5.1 Exercises (Integration by Parts)

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \sin^{-1}(2t) \, \dd t. \)

SOLUTION Using the integration by parts formula, \[\int u \, \dd v = uv - \int v \, \dd u,\] we let \(u = \sin^{-1}(2t)\) and \(\dd v = \, \dd t.\) Then, \(\displaystyle \dd u = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - 4t^2}} \, \dd t\) and \(v = t.\) After plugging in these expressions, we obtain \[ \begin{aligned} \int \sin^{-1}(2t) \, \dd t &= t \sin^{-1}(2t) - \int \frac{2t}{\sqrt{1 - 4t^2}} \, \dd t. \\ \end{aligned} \] We can evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2t}{\sqrt{1 - 4t^2}} \, \dd t\) using the substitution \(A = 1 - 4t^2.\) Then, \(\dd A = -8t \, \dd t\) and the integral becomes \[ \begin{aligned} &\int \frac{-1}{4\sqrt{A}} \, \dd A \\ = &-\frac{1}{4} \left(2 \sqrt{A}\right) + C \\ = &-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{1 - 4t^2} + C. \end{aligned} \] Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} \int \sin^{-1}(2t) \, \dd t &= t \sin^{-1}(2t) - \left(-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{1 - 4t^2}\right) + C \\ &= \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{t \sin^{-1}(2t) + \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{1 - 4t^2} + C.} \end{aligned} \]
