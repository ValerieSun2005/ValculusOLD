0.6 Exercises (Graphing Calculator Practice)

A graphing calculator is required for these exercises.

(1) Let \(\displaystyle f(x) = x^3 - \frac{2}{3}x^2 - 8x + 7\), and let \(\displaystyle g(x) = 30 - e^{5 - x} \). Find the intersection points of \(f\) and \(g\).

(2) Let \(\displaystyle f(x) = 23\sin{\left(\frac{80x}{77} - \frac{\pi}{32.55}\right)} \), and let \(\displaystyle g(x) = \log_3(x + 4) \). Find the intersection points of \(f\) and \(g\).

(3) Let \(\displaystyle p(x) = e^{2x - 3}\). At what value of \(x\) does \(p(x) = 4\) ?

(4) A ball is dropped, and its height as a function of time is given by \(h(t) = -16t^2 + 80t + 40\), where \(h(t)\) represents the height from the ground and \(t\) represents time, measured in seconds. After how many seconds is the ball \(50\) feet above the ground for the first time?

(5) Find the zeros of the function \(\displaystyle y = -10 \cos{\left(\frac{x}{12} - x^2\right)} \) on the interval \([-2,2]\).

(6) What are the minimum and maximum values of the function \(f(x) = x^3 + 4x^2 - 16x + 9\) for \(-2 \leq x \leq 4\) ?

(7) Let \(g(x) = x^2 + bx + 2\). Find the interval of the values of \(b\) for which \(g\) has no real solutions.

(8) The function \(x^2 - 10x + 8\) will be rewritten in the form \((x-p)^2 + q\). Find \(p + q\).

(9) Find the minimum point of the function \(h(x) = x^2 + 10x - 4.\)

(10) Let \(f(x) = -x^2 - 6x + 7\). Find the \(x\)-coordinate of the maximum point of \(f\).

(11) Let \(h(x) = x^2 + 4x - 6\). Which of the following statements is/are true?

I. \(h\) has two real solutions.

II. The solutions to \(h\) are \(-4 \pm \sqrt{10}\).

III. The coordinates of the maximum point of \(h\) are \((-2,10)\).
