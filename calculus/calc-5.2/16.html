5.2 Exercises — Problem 16

\((16)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \tan^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int \tan^2(x) \tan^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan^2(x) \left(\sec^2(x) - 1 \right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x - \int \tan^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x - \int \left(\sec^2(x) - 1\right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x + x - \tan(x) $$ Now, let \(u = \tan(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = \sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). We then have $$ \int \tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int u^2 \, \textrm{d} u$$ $$ = \frac{u^3}{3} + C $$ $$ = \frac{1}{3} \tan^3(x) + C. $$ Thus, the integral becomes $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{3} \tan^3(x) - \tan(x) + x + C.} $$
