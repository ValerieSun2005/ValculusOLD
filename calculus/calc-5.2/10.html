5.2 Exercises — Problem 10

\((10)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \cot^3(x) \csc^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral, using the Pythagorean identity, as $$ \int \cot(x) \left(\csc^2(x) - 1\right) \csc^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ Now, let \(u = \csc(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = -\csc(x) \cot(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). We now have $$ -\int u^3 \left(u^2 - 1\right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = -\int \left(u^5 - u^3\right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = \frac{u^4}{4} - \frac{u^6}{6} + C. $$ Substituting back gives $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{4} \csc^4(x) - \frac{1}{6} \csc^6(x) + C.} $$
