5.2 Exercises — Problem 4

\((4)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \cos^3(x) \sin^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

For this problem, rewrite the integral, using the Pythagorean identity, as $$ \int \cos(x) \left(1 - \sin^2(x) \right) \sin^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Now,

Let \(u = \sin(x)\).

$$ \textrm{d} u = \cos(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ The integral then becomes $$ \int \cancel{\cos(x)} \left(1 - u^2 \right) u^4 \Big(\frac{1}{\cancel{\cos(x)}}\Big) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = \int \left(u^4 - u^6 \right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = \frac{u^5}{5} - \frac{u^7}{7} + C. $$ Substituting back, we get $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{5} \sin^5(x) - \frac{1}{7} \sin^7(x) + C.} $$
