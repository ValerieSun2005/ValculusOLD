5.2 Exercises — Problem 9

\((9)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \csc^8(x) \cot^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral, using the Pythagorean identity, as $$ \int \cot^2(x) \csc^2(x) \csc^6(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ \int \cot^2(x) \csc^2(x) \left(1 + \cot^2(x) \right)^3 \, \textrm{d} x $$ Now, let \(u = \cot(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = -\csc^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). We now obtain $$ -\int u^2 \left(1 + u^2\right)^3 \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = -\int u^2 \left(u^6 + 3u^4 + 3u^2 + 1 \right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = -\int \left(u^8 + 3u^6 + 3u^4 + u^2 \right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = - \frac{u^9}{9} - \frac{3u^7}{7} - \frac{3u^5}{5} - \frac{u^3}{3} + C. $$ Substituting back gives $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{- \frac{1}{9} \cot^9(x) - \frac{3}{7} \cot^7(x) - \frac{3}{5} \cot^5(x) - \frac{1}{3} \cot^3(x) + C.} $$
