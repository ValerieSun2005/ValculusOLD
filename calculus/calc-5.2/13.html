5.2 Exercises — Problem 13

\((13)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int \csc(x) \csc^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Recall that $$ \int \csc^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x = -\cot(x). $$ Using the integration by parts formula, $$ \int f(x) g'(x) \, \textrm{d} x = f(x) g(x) - \int f'(x) g(x) \, \textrm{d} x, $$ we let \(\displaystyle f(x) = \csc(x) \) and \(\displaystyle g'(x) = \csc^2(x)\). Then, \(\displaystyle f'(x) = -\csc(x)\cot(x) \tan(x)\) and \(\displaystyle g(x) = -\cot(x)\). We then obtain $$ - \csc(x) \cot(x) - \int -\csc(x)\cot(x) \left(-\cot^2(x) \right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = - \csc(x) \cot(x) - \int \csc(x) \left(\csc^2(x) - 1\right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = - \csc(x) \cot(x) - \int \left(\csc^3(x) - \csc(x)\right) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ $$ = - \csc(x) \cot(x) - \int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x + \int \csc(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Thus, $$ \int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x = - \csc(x) \cot(x) - \int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x + \int \csc(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ 2\int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x = - \csc(x) \cot(x) + \int \csc(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ \int \csc^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x = \frac{1}{2} \left[ - \csc(x) \cot(x) + \int \csc(x) \, \textrm{d} x \right] $$ Recall that \(\displaystyle \int \csc(x) \, \textrm{d} x = - \ln{\left|\csc(x) + \cot(x) \right|} + C\). We then get—for our final answer— $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{-\frac{1}{2} \csc(x) \cot(x) - \frac{1}{2} \ln{\left|\csc(x) + \cot(x) \right|} + C.} $$
