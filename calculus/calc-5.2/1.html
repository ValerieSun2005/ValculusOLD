5.2 Exercises — Problem 1

\((1)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \sin(x) \cos^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

For this problem, we can perform the substitution \(u = \cos(x)\).

Let \(u = \cos(x)\).

$$ \textrm{d} u = - \sin(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ The integral then becomes $$ \int \cancel{\sin(x)} u^2 \Big(\frac{-1}{\cancel{\sin(x)}}\Big) \, \textrm{d} u. $$ $$ = - \int u^2 \, \textrm{d} u. $$ $$ = - \frac{u^3}{3} + C. $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{- \frac{1}{3} \cos^3(x) + C.} $$
