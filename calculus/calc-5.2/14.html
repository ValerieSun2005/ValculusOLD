5.2 Exercises — Problem 14

\((14)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \tan^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int \tan(x) \tan^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ $$ = \int \tan(x) \left(\sec^2(x) - 1\right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan(x) \sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x - \int \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Recall that \(\displaystyle \int \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x = \ln{\left|\sec(x) \right|} + C \).

For \(\displaystyle \int \tan(x) \sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x\), we can rewrite it as \(\displaystyle \int \tan(x) \sec(x) \sec(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). Now, let \(u = \sec(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = \sec(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). We then get $$ \int \tan(x) \sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x = \int u \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = \frac{u^2}{2} + C = \frac{1}{2} \sec^2(x) + C. $$

Thus, $$ \int \tan^3(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \tan^2(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{2} \sec^2(x) - \ln{\left|\sec(x) \right|} + C.} $$
