5.2 Exercises — Problem 20

\((20)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \left( \sin(x)\cos^2(8x) - \sin^2(8x) \sin(x) \right) \, \textrm{d} x \).

From the double-angle identity for cosine, we know that $$ \cos(2x) = \cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x). $$ Therefore, $$ \cos^2(8x) - \sin^2(8x) = \cos(16x). $$ The integral can be factored and rewritten as $$ \int \left( \sin(x) \left(\cos^2(8x) - \sin^2(8x) \right) \right) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \sin(x) \cos(16x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Further rewriting yields $$ \int \frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(x + 16x)) + \sin(x - 16x) \right] \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(17x) + \sin(-15x) \right] \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(17x) - \sin(15x) \right] \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{-\frac{1}{34} \cos(17x) + \frac{1}{30} \cos(15x) + C.} $$
