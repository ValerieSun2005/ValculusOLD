5.2 Exercises — Problem 15

\((15)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \sec^4(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int \sec^2(x) \left(1 + \tan^2(x)\right) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Now, let \(u = \tan(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = \sec^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). The integral then becomes $$ \int \left(1 + u^2\right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = u + \frac{u^3}{3} + C $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\tan(x) + \frac{1}{3} \tan^3(x) + C.} $$
