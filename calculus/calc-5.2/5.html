5.2 Exercises — Problem 5

\((5)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \sec^2(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

The integral can be rewritten as $$ \int \sec(x) \sec(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Recall that $$ \frac{\textrm{d}}{\textrm{d} x} \left[\sec(x)\right] = \sec(x) \tan(x). $$ Therefore, the substitution \(u = \sec(x)\) will help clear the \(\sec(x) \tan(x)\) term.

Let \(u = \sec(x)\).

$$ \textrm{d} u = \sec(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ We then get $$ \int \sec(x) \sec(x) \tan(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ $$ = \int u \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = \frac{u^2}{2} + C. $$ Substituting back, we obtain $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{2} \sec^2(x) + C.} $$
