5.2 Exercises — Problem 3

\((3)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \sin^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integrand using the power-reduction formula $$ \displaystyle \sin^2(x) = \frac{1}{2} \left[1 - \cos(2x)\right]. $$ We then have $$ \int \sin^2(x) \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{2} \left[1 - \cos(2x)\right] \, \textrm{d} x $$ $$ = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{\frac{1}{2} x - \frac{1}{4} \sin(2x) + C.} $$
