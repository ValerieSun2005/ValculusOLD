5.2 Exercises — Problem 8

\((8)\) Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \csc^4(x) \cot(x) \, \textrm{d} x \).

We can rewrite the integral as $$ \int \csc^3(x) \csc(x) \cot(x) \, \textrm{d} x. $$ Now, let \(u = \csc(x)\). Then, \(\textrm{d} u = -\csc(x) \cot(x) \, \textrm{d} x\). The integral then becomes $$ \int \left(-u^3\right) \, \textrm{d} u $$ $$ = -\frac{u^4}{4} + C. $$ Substituting back gives $$ \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{-\frac{1}{4} \csc^4(x) + C.} $$
