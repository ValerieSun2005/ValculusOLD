10.6 Exercises — Alternating Series

Click on a problem number to view the solution.

\(1.\)
Describe the conditions for the alternating series \(\ds \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \, b_n\) to be convergent.
\(2.\)
True or False: In the alternating series \(\ds \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \, b_n \, ,\) if \(\ds \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n \ne 0,\) then the series diverges.
\(3.\)
Consider the alternating series \(\ds \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \, b_n \, .\) To use the alternating series test, why might it be necessary to ensure that \(b_n\) is decreasing, even if \(\ds \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 0 \; ?\)
\(4.\)
Find a divergent series \(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty b_n\) such that \(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^n \, b_n\) is convergent.
\(5.\)
What are the conditions needed to use the alternating series error bound?
\(6.\)
The series \(\ds S = \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^n \, b_n\) is to be approximated using the partial sum \(\ds S_3 = \sum_{n = 1}^3 (-1)^n \, b_n \, .\) It is known that the terms of \(S\) are decreasing in magnitude to \(0.\) Show that \(\left| S - S_3 \right| \leq b_4 \, .\) What is \(b_4\) called in this context?

For each of exercises 7–10, determine whether the series is alternating.

\(7.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{3n + 18}}{n + 1} \)
\(8.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{\cos(\pi n)}{n^2} \)
\(9.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{(-2)^{2n - 1}}{n^{2/3} + 3} \)
\(10.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^{-2n + 4} \)

For each of exercises 11–16, determine whether the series converges or diverges.

\(11.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{n^{-2/3}}{(-1)^n} \)
\(12.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^{n - 1} \frac{n}{2n + 8} \)
\(13.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{n^3 - 8}{\cos(\pi n) \, n^2} \)
\(14.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{\sqrt{n + 1}}{n + 3} \)
\(15.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 2}^\infty \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2} + n \pi \right) \frac{n^2 - 4}{n^3 + 1} \)
\(16.\)
\(\ds \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^{n - 2} \frac{\sqrt{n} + 2n}{\sqrt{n + 1}} \)
