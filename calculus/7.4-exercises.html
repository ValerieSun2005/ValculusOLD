7.4 Exercises — Separation of Variables

(1) Find the solution to the differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{2} - 3\), given that \(y = 1\) when \(x = 0\).

(2) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' = -\sin{(x)} \), given that \(y(0) = 2\).

(3) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' -\cos{(2x)} = 0\) if the point \((0, \pi)\) passes through the solution curve.

(4) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' = e^{y}\) with the initial condition \((0, 1)\).

(5) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' - \sqrt{y} = 0\).

(6) Find the solution to the differential equation \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{1}{2t - e}\) with the initial condition \((e, 4)\).

(7) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' + 2y^2 + y = 0 \) with the initial condition \((3, 4)\).

(8) Let \(a, b,\) and \(c \, \) be constants in the differential equation \( y \, ' + ay^2 + by + c = 0 \). Find the general solution of the differential equation.

(9) Find the solution to the differential equation \(y \, ' = \frac{5y^2}{x\ln{(\ln{(x)})}}\) passing through the point \((e^2, 10)\).

(10) Let \(k\) be a constant in the differential equation \(\dot y + \frac{t^2}{2} - kt = 0\) such that the points \((0, 1)\) and \((1, 4)\) pass through the solution curve. Find the value of \(k\).

(11) Let \(b\) be a constant in the differential equation \(by \, ' + y = 0\). Find the value of \(b\), given that the solution to the differential equation passes through the points \((2, 8)\) and \((3,10)\).

(12) Money in a savings account grows at a rate proportional to the amount of money in the account at a given time \(t\). There are originally \(1500\) dollars in the account, and after a year there are \(1550\) dollars. How much money will be in the account after three years?

(13) People at a train station enter at a rate given by \(E(t) = t^2 - 2t + 6\) and leave at a rate given by \(L(t) = 15\sin{(t}) + 20\). There are originally \(500\) people in the train station. Find the expression for the number of people in the train terminal in terms of \(t\).

(14) Pigs enter a pen at a rate given by \(g(t) = \frac{50}{t+3}\) and exit at a rate given by \(h(t) = 2\sin{(2t)-3}\). At \(t = 0\) there are \(30\) pigs inside the pen. Model a function for the number of pigs in the pen at a given time \(t\).

(15) Newton's Law of Cooling states that \(\frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T-T_s)\), where \(T\) denotes the temperature of an object as a function of time \(t\), \(T_s\) denotes the temperature of the outside environment, and \(k\) is the heat transfer coefficient. Derive an expression for \(T\) as a function of \(t\).

(16) An object with mass \(m\) is pulled with a constant force \(F_a\) and experiences a drag force modeled by \(\beta v^2\), where \(\beta\) is a constant and \(v\) denotes the object's velocity. Derive an expression for the velocity of the object in terms of \(t\), \(F_a\), \(m\), and \(\beta\), as appropriate. (Hint: Apply Newton's Second Law to obtain \(F_a - \beta v^2 = mv \, ' \).)

(17) A falling object experiences air resistance modeled by \(kv\), where \(k\) is the drag coefficient of the object and \(v\) denotes the object's velocity. By Newton's Second Law, \(kv - 10m = m\frac{dv}{dt}\), where \(m\) denotes the mass of the object and \(t\) denotes time. Derive an expression for \(\lim_{t \to \infty} v(t)\) in terms of \(m\) and \(k\). (This is called the terminal velocity of the object, the maximum speed at which the object can fall on Earth.)

(18) A probability density function (PDF) is a function whose curve produces an area that gives the probability that a continuous random variable will fall within a range of values. This function of area is called a cumulative density function (CDF), defined as the integral of the PDF. Common examples of CDFs include Normal, \(t\)-, and \(\chi^2\)-distributions, which are fundamental tools for statistical inference. The fundamental property of probability density functions is that the area under them is always \(1\). Let \(p(x)\) denote a probability density function given by \(ke^{-kx}\) for \(x \geq 0\), where \(k\) is a constant. Find the CDF for \(p(x)\), which outputs the probability that a random variable will take a value less than or equal to \(x\).
