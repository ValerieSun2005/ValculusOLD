0.2 — Review of Quadratics

Let's do a review of some Pre-Calculus before jumping into any Calculus content. The first important topic is Polynomials, which I have split into two sections — 0.2 is only for Quadatics, and 0.3 is for general Polynomials. For a more in-depth section on Quadratics, visit my Algebra page.

Recall that a quadratic is the form \(ax^2 + bx + c \), and depending on the leading coefficient\((a)\) the parabola either opens upward or downward.

If \(a>0\), the parabola will open upward. If \(a<0\), the parabola will open downward.

To find the solutions to a quadratic, which are the points \(x\) at which \(ax^2 + bx +c = 0\), we use the quadratic formula: \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]

A quadratic with no real solutions does NOT touch the x-axis. A quadratic with 1 real solution touches the x-axis ONCE. A quadratic with 2 real solutions touches the x-axis TWICE.

Graph \(A\) has two real solutions; graph \(B\) has one real solution; graph \(C\) has no real solutions.

If there are 2 real solutions, \(b^2 - 4ac > 0\)

If there is 1 real solution, \(b^2 - 4ac = 0\)

If there are no real solutions, \(b^2 - 4ac < 0\)

The vertex of a parabola is its maximum or minimum. If \(a>0\), the vertex is the minimum. If \(a<0\), the vertex is the maximum. The \(x\)-coordinate of a vertex is given by the formula $$ x=\frac{-b}{2a}. $$
