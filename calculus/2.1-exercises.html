2.1 Exercises — Defining a Derivative

For exercises 1–7, rewrite each limit as a derivative.

(1) \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sin(x + h) - \sin(x)}{h}\)

(2) \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\cos(2x + 2h) - \cos(2x)}{h} \)

(3) \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\left(8 + h\right)^4 - (8)^4}{h} \)

(4) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} \)

(5) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \frac{\sin(x^2) - \sin{\left(\pi^2/4\right)}}{x - \pi/2} \)

(6) \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h} \left(\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{x + h}\right) \)

(7) \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h} \Big(\ln(e + h) - 1 \Big)\)

Exercises 8–14 are conceptual questions.

(8) In an ecosystem, a population of deer is modeled by the function \(N(t)\), where \(N\) represents the number of deer and \(t\) is time, measured in months. It is known that \(N'(5) = 30\). What is the best interpretation for this value?

(9) The amount of money in a bank is modeled by the function \(M(t)\), where \(M\) is measured in dollars and \(t\) is measured in years. It is known that \(M'(2) = -40\). What is the best interpretation for this value?

(10) A runner's position from the starting line is modeled by the function \(x(t)\), where \(x\) represents the distance from the starting line, measured in meters, and \(t\) denotes time, measured in seconds. It is known that \(x'(8) = 0\). What is the best interpretation for this value?

(11) People enter the subway station at a rate given by \(s(t)\), where \(s(t)\) is measured in people per minute and \(t\) is time, measured in minutes. It is known that \(s'(60) = 40\). What is the best interpretation for this value?

(12) The temperature of a cooling batch of cookies is modeled by the function \(T(t)\), where \(T(t)\) is measured in degrees Fahrenheit and \(t\) is measured in minutes. It is known that \(T'(2) = -48\). What is the best interpretation for this value?
