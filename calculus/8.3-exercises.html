8.3 Exercises — Polar Integration

A calculator is required for these problems.

(1) Find the area enclosed by one petal of the polar function \(f(\theta) = 1 - \sin(4\theta) \).

(2) Find the area enclosed by the cardioid \(r = 1 - \cos(\theta)\).

(3) Find the area enclosed by the petals of the polar function \(g(\theta) = 5\sin(5\theta) \).

(4) Let \(v(\theta) = 4 + 2\sin(\theta) \). Find the area of the region bounded by \(v\) and the polar axis.

(5) Let \(f(\theta) = \frac{\theta}{6} \) for \(0 \leq \theta \leq \pi\). Find the area of the region bounded by \(f\), the \(x\)-axis, and the \(y\)-axis.

(6) Find the area enclosed by the inner loop of the limacon \(r = 2 - 3\sin(\theta)\).

(7) Find the area enclosed by the inner loop of the limacon \(r = 1 - 8\sin(\theta)\).

(8) Find the area enclosed by the inner loop of the limacon \(r = 2 - 15\cos(\theta)\).

(9) Find the area enclosed by the inner loop of the limacon \(r = 16 - 30\sin(\theta)\).

(10) Find the area that is inside the function \(r_1 = 2\) and outside of \(r_2 = 2 - \sin(\theta)\).

(11) Find the area inside the polar curve \(r = 2\cos(\theta)\) and outside the polar curve \(r = \cos(\theta)\).

(12) Find the area bounded between the polar curves \(f(\theta) = 4 \) and \(g(\theta) = 5 - \sin(\theta)\).

(13) Find the area that is inside the polar curve \(r = 2\) and also inside the polar curve \(r = 2 + 2\cos(\theta) \).

(14) Let \(f(\theta) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\sin^2(\theta) - \cos^2(\theta)}}\). Find the area bounded by the line \(y = 2\) and the graph of \(f(\theta)\).

(15) Let \(r^2 = 4\cos(2\theta)\). This polar curve is called a lemniscate. Find the area of the region enclosed by one petal of the lemniscate.
