0.2 Exercises — Problem 8

(8) The function \(x^2 - 10x + 8\) will be rewritten in the form \((x-p)^2 + q\). Find \(p + q\).

To complete the square, we need to analyze the coefficient of the \(x\)-term. In this case it is \(-10\). We must then insert \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{-10}{2}\right)^2 = 25\) to allow the quadratic to be rewritten as a perfect square. $$ x^2 - 10x + 8$$ $$ \underbrace{x^2 - 10x + \underline{25}}_{(x-5)^2} - \underline{25} + 8 $$ $$ (x- 5)^2 - 17.$$ $$ \therefore p + q = 5 - 17 = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{-2.}$$
