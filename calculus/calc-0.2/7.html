0.2 Exercises — Problem 7

(7) Let \(g(x) = x^2 + bx + 2\). Find the interval of the values of \(b\) for which \(g\) has no real zeros.

Let \(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\). We know that if \(\Delta \lt 0\), then the quadratic has no real zeros. In this case, $$ \Delta = b^2 - 4(1)(2) = b^2 - 8. $$ Letting \(\Delta \lt 0\) gives $$ b^2 - 8 \lt 0 \implies $$ $$ -2 \sqrt{2} \lt b \lt 2 \sqrt{2} \, \, \, \, \, \, \, \text{OR} \, \, \, \, \, \, \cancel{b \lt -2 \sqrt{2} \, \, \, \text{and} \, \, \, b \gt 2 \sqrt{2}} .$$ For \(x \displaystyle \in \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{(-2 \sqrt{2}, 2 \sqrt{2})}\), the \(g(x) = 0\) has no real solutions.
