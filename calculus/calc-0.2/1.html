0.2 Exercises — Problem 1

(1) Find the values of \(x\) for which \(x^2 + 6x + 9 = 0\).

For this problem, notice that the quadratic may be factored as a perfect square: $$ x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)^2. $$ We notice that, when \( \displaystyle x = -3, x^2 + 6x + 9 = 0\). Therefore, \(\bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{x = -3}\) satisfies the equality.
