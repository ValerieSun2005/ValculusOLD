0.2 Exercises — Problem 10

(10) Let \(f(x) = -x^2 - 6x + 7\). Find the \(x\)-coordinate of the maximum point of \(f\).

\(f(x)\) is a parabola that opens downward, so the vertex represents the maximum point, in this case. The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is therefore given by $$ x = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{-6}{2(-1)} = \bbox[border: 2px solid white, 2pt]{-3.} $$
