0.2 Exercises — Problem 11

(11) Let \(h(x) = x^2 + 4x - 6\). Which of the following is true?

I. \(h\) has two real zeros.

II. The solutions to \(h\) are \(-4 \pm \sqrt{10}\).

III. The coordinates of the maximum point of \(h\) are \((-2,10)\).

We notice, from the quadratic formula, that the solutions to \(h(x) = 0\) are $$ x = \frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{4^2 - 4(1)(-6)}}{2(1)} = \frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{40}}{2}, $$ from which we immediately reject option II. and affirm I.

The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex of \(h\) is given by $$ x = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{4}{2} = -2. $$ The \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex is given by $$ f(-2) = (-2)^2 + 4(-2) -6 = -10, $$ so we accept options I and III.
